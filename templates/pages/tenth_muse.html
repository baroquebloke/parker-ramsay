Mission

The musicians of Tenth Muse have a very simple goal: to integrate music from the past with that of the present on period instruments. Inventive programming is at the core of the project, using alternative spaces around the campus at Oberlin College to combine early and contemporary music with spoken word and visual art composed by Obies. From Italian toccatas to minimalist works for harpsichords, to composing new works for period instruments and electronics, Tenth Muse is neither a “early music group” or a “contemporary music ensemble.” Instead, Tenth Muse invites listeners to be immersed into a cohesive musical experience, centered on artistic themes, musical ideas or even the spaces in which we are performing.
